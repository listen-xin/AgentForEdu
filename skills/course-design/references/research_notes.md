# Research Notes — Course Designer

## Evidence Summary

This skill is distilled from 24+ academic sources across instructional design, curriculum theory, and learning sciences. Core evidence pillars:

### Pillar 1: Constructive Alignment

Biggs & Tang's Constructive Alignment framework is the backbone of the objective-assessment-activity pipeline. The key operational insight: alignment breaks at three distinct interfaces, and the ILO→Assessment interface is the most damaging because of the backwash effect — students orient effort around assessment, not stated objectives.

### Pillar 2: Bloom's Revised Taxonomy

The cognitive process dimension (6 levels) combined with the knowledge dimension (4 types) provides a 24-cell matrix for precise objective specification. The single most impactful intervention is replacing non-observable verbs ("understand", "know") with specific observable verb pairs.

### Pillar 3: Active Learning Meta-Analysis

Freeman et al. (2014) establishes that active learning reduces failure rates by 1.5x. Deslauriers et al. (2019) reveals the critical gap between perceived and actual learning. The practical takeaway: lecture has a legitimate role for L1-L2 content; the art is knowing when to switch modalities.

### Pillar 4: UDL & Differentiation

CAST's UDL framework and Tomlinson's differentiation model converge on the same insight: standardize outcomes, differentiate pathways. The low-resource adaptations (multi-modal switching, choice boards, Bloom-tiered questioning) are validated by classroom implementation data.

### Pillar 5: Feedback & Formative Assessment

Hattie's meta-analysis (d=0.73 for feedback) combined with Black & Wiliam's formative assessment research and Nicol & Macfarlane-Dick's feedback principles establish that feedback system design (rubrics + peer review + rotation) outperforms individual feedback labor in both effectiveness and sustainability.

## Research Gaps

- **Cognitive load & threshold concepts** (R2 lost): Content chunking, spiral sequencing, and threshold concept identification are not covered in the current skill
- **Online/digital course design**: All findings are based on synchronous, face-to-face settings
- **Program-level coherence**: This skill addresses single-course design; multi-course curriculum mapping is out of scope
